How to Get to Lauterbrunnen by Train

By WoW Train · Updated June 2026 · 3 min read

View timetables and tickets on Trainline →

A valley of 72 waterfalls beneath sheer rock walls — including the Staubbach Falls, tumbling almost 300 metres straight off the cliff. Lauterbrunnen is the heart of the Swiss Bernese Oberland, and the train ride in is half the magic.

The secret no one tells you: there's no direct mainline train to Lauterbrunnen. You change at Interlaken Ost onto the narrow-gauge Berner Oberland Bahn (BOB) — a ~20-minute mountain railway that climbs into the valley with the cliffs rising around you. The connection is timed, and the ride itself is unforgettable.

What is the route via Interlaken?

The gateway is Interlaken Ost, reachable by fast, frequent SBB trains from Zürich, Bern, Basel and beyond (around 2 hours from Zürich). From Interlaken Ost, the Berner Oberland Bahn runs up to Lauterbrunnen in about 20 minutes.

How to make the connection?

  • 1. Get to Interlaken Ost (the Bernese Oberland hub).
  • 2. Change onto the Berner Oberland Bahn toward Lauterbrunnen (timed to connect).
  • 3. Arrive at Lauterbrunnen station, in the middle of the valley.
