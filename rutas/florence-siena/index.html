Florence to Siena by Train

By WoW Train · Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

Medieval Siena — its shell-shaped Piazza del Campo, the soaring Torre del Mangia and a striped marble cathedral — is one of Tuscany's great day trips, and the regional train from Florence puts it within easy reach.

The journey, stop by stop

From Firenze Santa Maria Novella regional trains head south through the Tuscan hills to Siena. Some departures run direct; others involve a quick change at Empoli. Either way the scenery — vineyards, cypress ridges and hilltop villages — is part of the appeal.

How long does it take?

Regional trains take around 1h30, running roughly hourly through the day. There's no high-speed option on this line, and no need to book far ahead. Check the live schedule for your date.

Getting from the station into Siena

One thing worth knowing: Siena's station sits about 2 km below the walled old town. A set of escalators (scale mobili) and frequent city buses carry you up to the centre in a few minutes. If arriving right in the heart of town matters to you, the direct bus from Florence (Autolinee Toscane / Flixbus) drops off closer to Piazza del Campo — but the train is faster and more scenic.

Who runs the route?

The route is run by Trenitalia Regionale. Regional fares are fixed and inexpensive, so there's no advantage to booking far in advance.

Florence to Siena train price (2026)

Standard second-class regional fares are around €10 each way, the same price whether you book ahead or on the day.

How to get the best fare

  • No need to rush the booking. Regional fares are fixed, so the price is the same on the day — just turn up and go.
  • Validate paper tickets. If you buy a paper regional ticket, stamp it in the green or white platform machines before boarding. Mobile tickets don't need validating.
  • Travel off-peak. Mid-morning and mid-week departures tend to be quieter.

The Siena day trip

Siena rewards a full day: watch the shadows move across the fan-shaped Piazza del Campo, climb the Torre del Mangia for rooftops all the way to the hills, and step inside the black-and-white striped Duomo. With a full day you can add nearby San Gimignano by regional bus, or make it a two-stop Tuscan loop with Pisa.
