Paris to Bruges by Train

By WoW Train · Updated July 2026 · 4 min read

The fairytale canals of Bruges are an easy day trip or weekend escape from Paris, reached with one quick change in Brussels — no need to fly or drive.

The journey, stop by stop

From Paris Gare du Nord a high-speed Thalys or Eurostar train runs to Brussels-Midi in around 1h25m. From there, a frequent InterCity service continues west through Ghent to Bruges.

How long does it take?

Total journey time is around 2h 50m to 3h 10m, depending on the connection at Brussels-Midi. Check the live schedule for your date.

Who runs the route?

The Paris–Brussels leg runs on Thalys/Eurostar, and the Brussels–Bruges leg on SNCB/NMBS, Belgium's national railway.

Paris to Bruges train price (2026)

Combined fares start from around €45–55 when booked ahead — the Brussels–Bruges leg is inexpensive on top of the high-speed ticket.

How to get the best fare

  • Book the high-speed leg early. Thalys/Eurostar fares rise fast as departure approaches.
  • Buy the Bruges leg separately once you know your Brussels arrival time — SNCB fares are flat and inexpensive.
  • Leave a comfortable connection window in Brussels-Midi, especially with luggage.
