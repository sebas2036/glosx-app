Prague to Budapest by Train: Direct Route & Travel Time

By WoW Train · Updated June 2026 · 5 min read

The Prague to Budapest train travel time is approximately 6 hours 40 minutes on the direct EuroCity service — no changes required. A classic Central European journey through Bohemia, Moravia and along the Danube, linking two of the continent's most beautiful capitals.

The route, stop by stop

Trains depart from Praha hlavní nádraží (Prague Main Station), the city's grand central terminus. The route heads southeast across Moravia, stopping at Brno — Czech Republic's second city — before crossing into Slovakia and passing near Bratislava. After the Slovak capital, the train crosses into Hungary and follows the Danube valley into Budapest-Nyugati, the city's iconic Western Station designed by Gustave Eiffel's firm.

The journey through southern Moravia and along the Danube offers genuine scenery — rolling hills, vineyards and river valleys that make the long ride feel worthwhile.

Journey time and schedule

The fastest direct trains take approximately 6 hours 40 minutes. Most departures are in the morning, with 3 to 4 trains per day. The schedule varies slightly by season and day of the week, so always check the live timetable for your exact date before booking.

Operators and tickets

The Prague–Budapest corridor is served by a joint EuroCity operation between ČD (Czech Railways), ÖBB (Austrian Federal Railways) and MÁV (Hungarian State Railways). You can book through any of them, but using a single European search platform is the easiest way to compare all departures and find the best fare.

Prague to Budapest train price (2026)

Railjet tickets start from around €19 booked in advance. Standard fares are typically €40–70.

How to get the cheapest ticket

  • Book as early as possible. Saver fares (€19–€30) are limited and sell out weeks in advance, especially in summer.
  • Travel mid-week. Monday–Thursday departures are typically less busy and cheaper than weekends.
  • Avoid school holidays. July–August and Czech/Hungarian national holidays see significant price spikes.
  • Consider a railpass if you're combining this with other Central European routes — it can work out cheaper across multiple legs.
  • First class on EuroCity is often only €10–€15 more and worth it for a 6h+ journey.

Arriving in Budapest

Budapest-Nyugati (Western Station) sits right in the heart of Pest, on the Grand Boulevard. Metro line M3 (blue) runs directly from the station to the city centre. Taxis and rideshare apps are available outside the main entrance.

If your hotel is in Buda, the easiest connection is the M3 to Deák Ferenc tér, then tram 2 along the Danube embankment.

Departing from Prague

Praha hlavní nádraží is connected to the metro (line C, red line) and is central to most accommodation. Arrive at least 15–20 minutes before departure — EuroCity trains board from the main platforms and reservations are assigned specific seats.

Frequently asked questions

How long is the train from Prague to Budapest?

The direct EuroCity train takes approximately 6 hours 40 minutes. Some services routed via Bratislava may add a few minutes. There is no need to change trains.

How much does a Prague to Budapest train ticket cost?

Tickets start from around €19 when booked in advance. Standard fares typically range from €30 to €60. Buying several weeks ahead gives the best prices.

Is there a direct train from Prague to Budapest?

Yes. EuroCity trains operated by ČD, ÖBB and MÁV run directly, with no change of train required. Some services run via Bratislava.

How many trains run between Prague and Budapest per day?

Typically 3 to 4 direct departures per day. Morning trains are the most popular. Check the live schedule for your date as frequencies can vary seasonally.

Which stations do I use?

Trains depart from Praha hlavní nádraží (Prague Main Station) and arrive at Budapest-Nyugati (Western Station). Both are centrally located with metro connections.
