Paris to London by Train

By WoW Train · Updated June 2026 · 4 min read

Two of Europe's great capitals, joined under the sea. Eurostar slips out of central Paris, dives through the Channel Tunnel and surfaces in the heart of London — city centre to city centre, no airport in sight.

The journey, stop by stop

You leave from Gare du Nord, glide across the plains of northern France and Picardy, then enter the Channel Tunnel for the 35-kilometre crossing beneath the sea. On the English side the train runs through Kent and arrives at the restored Victorian arches of London St Pancras International.

How long does it take?

The fastest direct trains cover the route in around 2h 20m, with several departures a day. Exact times shift by service and season, so check the live schedule for your date before you lock in plans.

Who runs the route?

The route is served by Eurostar. Comparing the day's departures in one search is the easiest way to find the best time and fare.

Paris to London train price (2026)

Eurostar tickets start from around €39 booked well in advance. Standard fares typically range €80–150, and last-minute seats can exceed €300.

How to get the best fare

  • Book early. The cheapest saver fares sell out first — booking ahead can be dramatically cheaper than buying on the day.
  • Travel off-peak. Mid-morning and mid-week departures tend to be quieter and cheaper.
  • Consider first class — on many routes the upgrade is modest and very comfortable.
  • Compare in one place to see every departure at a glance.

Why take the train instead of flying?

Flying Paris–London means two airports far from the centre, security queues and the same sea to cross. Eurostar drops you in zone 1 of each city with a single, calm ride.
