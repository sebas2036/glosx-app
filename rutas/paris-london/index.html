Paris to London by Train

By WoW Train · Updated June 2026 · 4 min read

Two of Europe's great capitals, joined under the sea. Eurostar slips out of central Paris, dives through the Channel Tunnel and surfaces in the heart of London — city centre to city centre, no airport in sight.

The journey, stop by stop

You leave from Gare du Nord, glide across the plains of northern France and Picardy, then enter the Channel Tunnel for the 35-kilometre crossing beneath the sea. On the English side the train runs through Kent and arrives at the restored Victorian arches of London St Pancras International.

How long does it take?

The fastest direct trains cover the route in around 2h 20m, with several departures a day. Exact times shift by service and season, so check the live schedule for your date before you lock in plans.

Who runs the route?

The route is served by Eurostar. Comparing the day's departures in one search is the easiest way to find the best time and fare.

Paris to London train price (2026)

Eurostar tickets start from around €39 booked well in advance. Standard fares typically range €80–150, and last-minute seats can exceed €300.

How to get the best fare

  • Book early. The cheapest saver fares sell out first — booking ahead can be dramatically cheaper than buying on the day.
  • Travel off-peak. Mid-morning and mid-week departures tend to be quieter and cheaper.
  • Consider first class — on many routes the upgrade is modest and very comfortable.

Why take the train instead of flying?

Flying Paris–London means two airports far from the centre, security queues and the same sea to cross. Eurostar drops you in zone 1 of each city with a single, calm ride.

On board the Eurostar

The Eurostar e320 trains are a significant upgrade from the classic fleet. Standard seats are wide and comfortable with power sockets at every seat, free Wi-Fi throughout the journey and a dedicated bar-buffet car. The ride is smooth and quiet — the only dramatic moment is when the train plunges into the Channel Tunnel at Folkestone, marked by a brief drop in cabin pressure and total darkness outside the windows for about 20 minutes.

Business Premier passengers get dedicated check-in, a three-course meal at their seat and access to the lounge at St Pancras. Standard Premier sits between — a wider seat with meal service, ideal for those who want comfort without the full business price.

Station guide: Gare du Nord

Gare du Nord is the busiest railway station in Europe and handles Eurostar, TGV, Thalys and regional trains. The Eurostar departure hall is on the upper level — allow at least 45 minutes before departure for check-in, passport control (UK border, done in Paris) and security. The station has a full range of shops, cafés and a large Relay newsagent for last-minute supplies.

Getting to Gare du Nord from central Paris: Metro line 4 or 5 (direct from Châtelet or République), or the RER B from CDG airport (about 25 minutes). Uber and taxis drop off on the Rue de Maubeuge side.

Station guide: London St Pancras International

St Pancras is one of London's most beautiful stations — the restored Victorian gothic façade houses a champagne bar, restaurants and independent shops. Arrivals clear UK Border Force on the lower level. The station connects directly to King's Cross next door, giving you access to the Northern line, Victoria line, Piccadilly line (for Heathrow) and the Elizabeth line. City centre hotels are reachable in 10–15 minutes by Tube.

Best time to travel Paris to London

The cheapest fares appear in January–March and October–November (outside school holidays). Peak prices hit in July–August, over Christmas and around Easter. Mid-week morning departures (Tuesday–Thursday, before 10:00) are consistently cheaper than Fridays and Sundays. Tips for this route

  • Arrive 45 minutes early. Eurostar checks in like a short-haul flight — passport control in Paris is UK border, so late arrival means a missed train with no flexibility.
  • Bring your passport, not just an ID card, if you hold a UK passport. EU citizens can use their national ID card.
  • Luggage: No size limit and no fee — just what you can carry yourself. There are overhead racks and end-of-carriage luggage areas.
  • Eurostar app: Download your ticket to your phone before travelling — the Wi-Fi in the Tunnel is intermittent.
  • Return trips: The London → Paris morning trains are often cheaper than the evening ones. Book your return at the same time as your outward journey for the best combined price.
