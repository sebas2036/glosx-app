Madrid to Barcelona by Train

By WoW Train · Updated June 2026 · 4 min read

Spain's flagship high-speed line connects the capital and the Mediterranean in two and a half hours at up to 300 km/h.

The journey, stop by stop

From Madrid Puerta de Atocha the AVE races across the plains of Castilla–La Mancha and Aragón, calling at Zaragoza, before descending toward the coast and arriving at Barcelona Sants.

How long does it take?

The fastest direct trains cover the route in around 2h 30m, with several departures a day. Exact times shift by service and season, so check the live schedule for your date before you lock in plans.

Who runs the route?

The route is served by Renfe AVE (also iryo and Ouigo). Comparing the day's departures in one search is the easiest way to find the best time and fare.

Madrid to Barcelona train price (2026)

Tickets start from around €14 with Ouigo, €20 with Iryo and €29 with Renfe AVE when booked in advance. Last-minute fares can reach €80–120 on the same journey.

How to get the best fare

  • Book early. The cheapest saver fares sell out first — booking ahead can be dramatically cheaper than buying on the day.
  • Travel off-peak. Mid-morning and mid-week departures tend to be quieter and cheaper.
  • Consider first class — on many routes the upgrade is modest and very comfortable.

Why take the train instead of flying?

With three operators competing on the line, fares are often cheaper than flying — and you arrive in the city centre with no airport transfer.

On board the AVE

The AVE (Alta Velocidad Española) runs on dedicated high-speed track and reaches speeds of up to 300 km/h. Renfe's Velaro trains feature comfortable reclining seats, power sockets and a cafeteria car. The ride is silky smooth — the main challenge is that the scenery in central Spain is flat and semi-arid, which is part of the dramatic Castilian landscape but not the most visually exciting section of rail travel in Spain. The approach into Barcelona through the Catalan hills is more rewarding.

Ouigo Spain and Iryo also run high-speed services on this route at lower prices, particularly if you book well in advance. Ouigo is no-frills (you pay for luggage and seat selection), while Iryo positions itself as a premium competitor to Renfe. All three operators use the same high-speed track and have similar journey times.

Station guide: Madrid Puerta de Atocha

Atocha is Madrid's main intercity rail hub and one of Europe's most distinctive stations — the old Victorian terminal houses a tropical garden inside. The high-speed platforms are in the modern extension (Atocha-Cercanías). Getting to Atocha: Metro line 1 (Atocha Renfe stop), or a short taxi/Uber from the city centre (around €10–15). The AVE check-in is security-controlled — arrive at least 20 minutes before departure.

Station guide: Barcelona Sants

Barcelona Sants is the main high-speed rail hub for Barcelona, though many AVE services also stop at Barcelona Passeig de Gràcia in the heart of the city — often a more convenient arrival point. Check your ticket: Passeig de Gràcia saves 15–20 minutes compared to Sants plus a Metro ride. Sants connects directly to Metro lines 3 and 5, and to the Aerobus link for the airport.

Best time to travel Madrid to Barcelona

Advance fares (booked 6–8 weeks ahead) start from around €15 with Ouigo, €20 with Renfe or Iryo. Summer is busy but multiple departures mean seats are available — the price is higher, not the availability. Avoid Friday evenings and Sunday afternoons (commuter peak). Mid-week morning services offer the best combination of price and seat availability.

Tips for this route

  • Three operators, three price points: always check Ouigo, Iryo and Renfe separately (or use Klook to compare all at once) — the cheapest operator can vary by date.
  • Ouigo luggage fees: Ouigo charges for hold luggage. If you're travelling light with carry-on only, it's the cheapest option. Otherwise Renfe's included baggage makes it competitive.
  • Check the stop: some services stop at Zaragoza-Delicias (about halfway) before continuing to Barcelona. This adds 20–30 minutes to the total journey.
  • Seat direction: when booking Renfe, you can choose forward or backward-facing. Forward-facing (in the direction of travel) gives slightly better views on approach to Barcelona.
  • Return same day: it's perfectly feasible to do Madrid–Barcelona as a long day trip (leave 07:00, back by 22:00) with a full day in either city.
