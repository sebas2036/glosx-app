Cheap Train Tickets Europe 2026

By WoW Train · July 2026 · 8 min read

European rail is a premium experience — but it doesn't have to be a premium price. In 2026, it's possible to cross the continent by high-speed train for less than the cost of a budget flight once you factor in baggage fees and airport transfers. Here's exactly how.

The 8 Strategies That Actually Work

1

Book advance fares, not flexible tickets

Every major European operator offers non-refundable advance fares that are 50–80% cheaper than walk-up prices. On a Paris–Lyon Eurostar you might pay €89 flexible or €10 advance. The rule: if you know your plans, always book ahead and take the advance fare.

2

Book at the 90-day window opening

Advance fares typically go on sale exactly 90 days before departure (some operators open at 120 days). The cheapest seats release at that moment — set a calendar reminder and book the morning they open for peak summer dates.

3

Use budget rail operators where available

Budget high-speed operators offer much lower base fares than the national carrier on the same routes: Ouigo in France and Spain (from €10), Italo in Italy (from €9.90), FlixTrain in Germany (from €9.99). The trade-off is fewer stations served and stricter luggage rules — check before booking.

4

Try split ticketing

On some routes, buying two separate tickets — e.g. Paris–Lyon + Lyon–Marseille instead of Paris–Marseille — is cheaper than a single through ticket. Use Klook to compare the through price vs. split, especially on longer journeys with a logical midpoint.

5

Travel off-peak

Friday afternoons, Sunday evenings and Monday mornings command a premium. Tuesday–Thursday departures outside school holidays are consistently the cheapest days. Adjust departure by a day or two and save 20–40%.

6

Use youth and senior discounts

Most European operators offer substantial discounts for travellers under 26 and over 60. SNCF's "Carte Jeune" gives 30% off for 12 months. DB's "BahnCard 25" pays for itself in 2–3 trips. SNCF's "Carte Senior" gives 30% off from age 60. These cards cost €20–50 and are worth it if you'll travel more than twice.

7

Consider overnight trains for double value

A couchette berth at €49–79 replaces both the transport and a night in a hotel. On a Vienna–Brussels route, a cheap couchette + saved accommodation easily undercuts the cheapest flight + hostel combination, especially in summer.

8

Passes vs. Point-to-Point: A Quick Decision

The question we get asked most: Is a Eurail pass cheaper? The short answer: for most planned trips of 2 weeks or less, individual advance tickets are cheaper.
