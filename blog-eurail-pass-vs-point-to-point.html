When to Buy a Eurail Pass

  • You're visiting 5 or more countries in a single trip
  • You're travelling spontaneously without fixed dates тАФ flexibility has real value
  • You're booking last-minute (within 2 weeks) when cheap advance tickets are gone
  • You want to take many short regional hops where no reservation is required
  • Your itinerary includes lots of Germany, Austria or Switzerland where pass travel is often reservation-free

When to Buy Point-to-Point Tickets

  • You're visiting 2 to 4 countries with a mostly fixed itinerary
  • You book at least 4 to 6 weeks in advance
  • Your main routes are high-speed (Eurostar, TGV, AVE, Frecciarossa) тАФ the mandatory reservation fees eat into a pass's value
  • You're travelling during shoulder season (AprilтАУMay, SeptemberтАУOctober) when cheap advance fares are plentiful
  • You want to use budget rail operators like Ouigo (France/Spain) which don't accept passes at all

Pro tip: Ouigo, the budget high-speed operator in France and Spain, does not accept Eurail or Interrail passes. A Paris to Lyon on Ouigo can cost as little as тВм10 тАФ far cheaper than any pass option for that leg.
